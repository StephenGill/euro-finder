European Structural and Investment Funds

SME mentoring: call for project proposals

Published:
19 January 2015
Closes:
31 March 2015
Type:
Business support
Area:
Dudley, Sandwell, Walsall and Wolverhampton (part of West Midlands)
Value:
Between £1 million and £5 million

Call to run a project providing mentoring to small and medium sized enterprises (SMEs) in the Black Country.

Details

In total, we are looking to give 1,000 SMEs access to mentors with expertise in:

  • attracting investment
  • taking on more employees - how to deal with the admin challenges
  • getting ready to export

Value

Funding available: up to £2 million.

Deadlines

Outline application deadline: 31 March 2015.
Full application deadline: 1 May 2015.
Project start date: no later than 1 January 2016.

Area covered by project

The project must cover all of the Black Country Local Enterprise Partnership area (Dudley, Sandwell, Walsall and Wolverhampton).

Completion date

The project must be completed no later than 31 December 2020.

Funding limits

You can apply for funding to cover up to 49% of the project cost, up to a maximum of £2 million. At least 51% of the project cost must be funded from a source other than the European Community.

Other requirements

You won’t get funding if your project:

  • duplicates activities that already exist
  • could be funded by the commercial market

How to apply

The first step is to make an outline application.

As part of your outline application, you’ll be asked to provide:

information on how your project meets European funding priorities and objectives a view on whether funding for the project would comply with the EU rules on state aid (some state aid is against EU rules because it makes markets less competitive - if you’re not sure, you should get legal advice)

Projects must meet the following European funding priorities and objectives:

enhancing the competitiveness of small and medium sized enterprises (SMEs) increasing growth capacity of SMEs

You can find details of European funding priorities and objectives in the European Regional Development Fund Operational Programme 2014 to 2020.

What happens next

If your outline application is successful, you’ll be asked to make a full application.

You’ll be asked for more detailed information as part of the full application. You may also be asked to provide financial guarantees.

Contact details

If you have a question, contact [add contact details].
